What You'll Learn

Ten days. Real skills. Job done.

Here's what you cover across the two weeks. Every session is hands-on. You don't move on until you've got it right — that's how we make sure you leave knowing what you're doing, not just holding a card.

Getting to know the machine

Before you dig an inch you need to know what you're sitting in. Pre-start checks, controls, how it moves, what can go wrong. We make sure you're comfortable before the real work starts.

Basic digging — done properly

Everyone starts here. Digging, levelling, loading. Simple tasks, but you'd be surprised how many operators cut corners. We don't let you move on until you're doing it right, every time.

Trenching & grading

This is what foremen actually need. Accurate trenches for drainage and utilities. Clean grades. The stuff that keeps you in work once you're out there.

Site safety — no fluff

What you're legally responsible for. Where the dangers actually are. Overhead cables, buried services, safe working zones. Real stuff, not just a tick-box exercise.

Loading & slewing

Speed and accuracy working together. Loading dumpers cleanly, slewing without spilling, keeping the site moving. This is what separates a good operator from a slow one.

Forward & rear tipping dumpers

On top of the excavators, you'll get time on the full dumper fleet — both forward and rear tippers. Two machines on your CPCS card means more sites will want you.

CPCS exam — you're ready

By the time you sit the exam, you've been doing this for two weeks on real machines. It's not a surprise. You know what's coming, you've done it already, and you walk in confident.
